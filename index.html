Metaphysics

"The unexamined life is not worth living." — Socrates

Metaphysics explores the fundamental nature of reality, existence, and the universe. It asks questions about being, substance, causality, and the relationship between mind and matter.

5 Main Ideas

  • Ontology (study of being)
  • Substance and Form
  • Causality and Determinism
  • Free Will vs. Fate
  • Mind-Body Problem

5 Key Thinkers

  • Aristotle
  • Plato
  • Immanuel Kant
  • Martin Heidegger
  • René Descartes

5 Big Questions

  • What is the nature of reality?
  • Does God exist?
  • What is time?
  • Are there universals?
  • What is consciousness?

Epistemology

"I think, therefore I am." — René Descartes

Epistemology is the study of knowledge, belief, and justification. It examines how we know what we know and the limits of human understanding.

5 Main Ideas

  • Justified True Belief
  • Rationalism vs. Empiricism
  • Skepticism
  • Foundationalism
  • Coherentism

5 Key Thinkers

  • Plato
  • John Locke
  • Immanuel Kant
  • David Hume
  • Bertrand Russell

5 Big Questions

  • What is knowledge?
  • Can we trust our senses?
  • Is certainty possible?
  • How do we distinguish truth from belief?
  • What is the role of reason?

Political Philosophy

"Man is by nature a political animal." — Aristotle

Political Philosophy examines the nature of government, justice, rights, and the ideal organization of society.

5 Main Ideas

  • Social Contract
  • Justice and Fairness
  • Liberty vs. Equality
  • Power and Authority
  • Democracy vs. Other Systems

5 Key Thinkers

  • Plato
  • John Locke
  • Jean-Jacques Rousseau
  • John Rawls
  • Thomas Hobbes

5 Big Questions

  • What is a just society?
  • What are the limits of state power?
  • Is democracy the best form of government?
  • Do individuals have natural rights?
  • When is rebellion justified?

Logic

"Contradictions do not exist. Whenever you think you are facing a contradiction, check your premises." — Ayn Rand

Logic studies the principles of valid reasoning and argumentation. It is the foundation of clear thinking and philosophical inquiry.

5 Main Ideas

  • Deductive vs. Inductive
  • Fallacies
  • Propositional Logic
  • Predicate Logic
  • Validity and Soundness

5 Key Thinkers

  • Aristotle
  • Gottlob Frege
  • Bertrand Russell
  • Ludwig Wittgenstein
  • Kurt Gödel

5 Big Questions

  • What makes an argument valid?
  • Can logic be complete?
  • How do we detect fallacies?
  • What is the relationship between language and logic?
  • Are there limits to reason?

Ethics

"Act only according to that maxim whereby you can at the same time will that it should become a universal law." — Immanuel Kant

Ethics investigates questions of morality, right and wrong, virtue, and how we ought to live.

5 Main Ideas

  • Virtue Ethics
  • Deontology
  • Utilitarianism
  • Meta-ethics
  • Applied Ethics

5 Key Thinkers

  • Aristotle
  • Immanuel Kant
  • John Stuart Mill
  • Friedrich Nietzsche
  • Peter Singer

5 Big Questions

  • What is the good life?
  • Are moral truths absolute?
  • How do we resolve moral dilemmas?
  • What are our duties to others?
  • Is morality culturally relative?

Aesthetics

"Beauty is truth, truth beauty." — John Keats

Aesthetics explores the nature of beauty, art, taste, and the philosophy of sensory experience.

5 Main Ideas

  • The Sublime
  • Art and Representation
  • Aesthetic Judgment
  • Expression Theory
  • Form and Content

5 Key Thinkers

  • Plato
  • Immanuel Kant
  • Friedrich Nietzsche
  • Arthur Schopenhauer
  • John Dewey

5 Big Questions

  • What is beauty?
  • Is art subjective or objective?
  • What is the purpose of art?
  • Can we define good taste?
  • How does art relate to truth?
