Ethics

Ethics is the philosophical study of moral principles and values that govern human behavior and judgments about what ought to be done.
Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, Immanuel Kant, John Stuart Mill
"The unexamined life is not worth living." — Socrates

Five Main Ideas

  • Utilitarianism
  • Deontology
  • Virtue Ethics
  • Moral Relativism
  • Eudaimonia

Five Big Questions

  • What is the idea of a good life?
  • Are moral truths absolute?
  • How do we resolve moral dilemmas?
  • What are our duties to others?
  • Is morality culturally relative?

Political Philosophy

Political Philosophy is the normative discipline that analyses how we ought to live together and examines the ideas of power.
Plato, Aristotle, John Locke, Niccolo Machiavelli, Karl Marx
"The price good men pay for indifference to public affairs is to be ruled by evil men." — Plato

Five Main Ideas

  • The Social Contract
  • Liberty
  • Rights
  • Authority & Legitimacy
  • Justice

Five Big Questions

  • What is justice?
  • Why should political authority be obeyed?
  • What is the relationship between the individual and the community?
  • What is equality?
  • Who should rule and for what purpose?

Aesthetics

Aesthetics is the branch of philosophy concerned with beauty, art, taste, and sensory experience.
Plato, Immanuel Kant, Friedrich Nietzsche, John Dewey, Arthur Schopenhauer
"Nothing is beautiful, only man: on this piece of naive rests all aesthetics, it is the first truth of aesthetics." — Friedrich Nietzsche

Five Main Ideas

  • Beauty
  • Sublime
  • Expression Theory
  • Art as Representation
  • Aesthetic Judgment

Five Big Questions

  • What is Beauty?
  • Is Art Subjective or Objective?
  • What is the Purpose of Art?
  • Can We Define Good Taste?
  • How Does Art Relate to Truth?

Metaphysics

Metaphysics is the branch of philosophy concerned with the fundamental nature of reality, existence, and being.
Plato, Aristotle, Immanuel Kant, René Descartes, Swami Vivekananda
"Why are there beings at all, instead of nothing?" — Martin Heidegger

Five Main Ideas

  • Ontology
  • Mind-Body Problem & Consciousness
  • Theory of Forms
  • Free Will vs Determinism
  • Reality vs Appearance

Five Big Questions

  • What is the fundamental nature of reality?
  • What is the relationship between mind and body?
  • Why is there something rather than nothing?
  • What is the nature of the self and personal identity?
  • Do humans have free will?

Epistemology

Epistemology is the branch of philosophy concerned with the nature, origin, and limits of human knowledge.
Plato, René Descartes, John Locke, David Hume, Immanuel Kant
"The human understanding, when it has once adopted an opinion, draws all things else to support and agree to it." — Francis Bacon

Five Main Ideas

  • Justified True Belief
  • Empiricism
  • Rationalism
  • Skepticism
  • A Priori and A Posteriori

Five Big Questions

  • What is knowledge?
  • Can we trust our senses?
  • Is certainty possible?
  • How do we distinguish truth from belief?
  • What is the role of reason?

Logic

Logic is the study of the methods and principles used to distinguish correct reasoning from incorrect reasoning.
Aristotle, Gottlob Frege, Uddayana, Bertrand Russell, Ludwig Wittgenstein
"Logic pervades the world; the limits of the world are also its limits." — Ludwig Wittgenstein

Five Main Ideas

  • Deductive vs. Inductive
  • Fallacies
  • Propositional Logic
  • Predicate Logic
  • Validity and Soundness

Five Big Questions

  • What makes an argument valid?
  • Can logic be complete?
  • How do we detect fallacies?
  • What is the relationship between language and logic?
  • Are there limits to reason?
